# Targeting: QA (Quality Assurance)

## Layer 1: Problem Definition

### What specific problem do we solve?

Customer service teams are manually reviewing a tiny fraction of interactions (typically 1-3%), leaving the vast majority of conversations unscored. QA managers and team leads have no visibility into systemic quality issues, agent coaching needs, or performance trends — they're making decisions based on a statistically insignificant sample that's often biased toward complaints or escalations.

### Evidence a company has this problem

- **Low QA coverage rates**: Job postings or team structures suggesting manual QA with small teams relative to agent headcount (e.g., 2 QA analysts for 200+ agents)
- **Spreadsheet-based QA**: References to Excel/Google Sheets scorecards, manual evaluation forms, or homegrown tools in job descriptions or employee profiles
- **High agent attrition / inconsistent CSAT**: Public Glassdoor reviews mentioning lack of feedback or coaching; visible CSAT inconsistency across agents on review sites
- **Large contact centre headcount**: Companies with 50+ customer-facing agents — the manual QA model breaks down at scale
- **Multiple communication channels**: Companies handling calls, chat, email, and tickets — each channel multiplies the QA burden

### Evidence they're actively trying to solve it

- Job postings for "QA Manager", "Quality Analyst", or "QA Lead" roles — they're investing in the function
- Job descriptions mentioning QA tooling evaluation, "QA transformation", or "QA automation"
- Recent hiring of a Head of CX, VP of Operations, or similar leadership role (often triggers a QA overhaul)
- Mentions of NPS/CSAT improvement initiatives in company blogs, earnings calls, or press releases
- Active evaluations or reviews of competing QA platforms (G2, Gartner Peer Insights activity)

## Layer 2: Solution Fit

### How else could they solve this problem?

- **Manual QA teams** — hire more analysts to increase coverage (expensive, doesn't scale)
- **Native platform QA** — use built-in QA features from Zendesk, Genesys, etc. (limited, rigid)
- **Competing QA platforms** — MaestroQA, Scorebuddy, EvaluAgent, Klaus (now part of Zendesk)
- **Build internally** — custom dashboards and scoring tools (engineering cost, maintenance burden)

### When is Rulebase's approach superior?

- When the company needs **100% coverage** across all interactions, not just a sample — Rulebase's AI evaluates every conversation automatically
- When they want **customisable scorecards** that map to their specific quality criteria, not a one-size-fits-all template
- When they need QA that works across **multiple channels and platforms** (calls, tickets, chat) from a single pane of glass
- When they want **AI + human QA working together** — AI handles volume, humans handle nuance, contested evaluations have a resolution workflow
- When they need QA tightly coupled with **compliance and risk detection** rather than standalone quality scoring

### Evidence of these scenarios

- Multi-platform tech stack (Aircall + Zendesk, or similar combinations) visible in job posts or BuiltWith/Slintel
- Companies publicly discussing AI adoption for CX operations
- High interaction volumes (visible from headcount, customer base size, or support ticket volume mentioned in case studies)
- Regulated industry where QA and compliance are intertwined (financial services, insurance, healthcare)

## Layer 3: Economic Drivers

### Where does this problem have the highest cost of inaction?

- **Regulated industries** (financial services, insurance) — poor QA leads to compliance failures, fines, and enforcement actions
- **High-volume contact centres** (100+ agents) — the gap between 2% and 100% QA coverage represents massive blind spots in agent performance
- **Companies with high agent attrition** — without QA-driven coaching, attrition stays high and training costs compound
- **Businesses where CX is a competitive differentiator** — fintech, SaaS, D2C brands where CSAT/NPS directly impacts retention and LTV

### Drivers of larger deal sizes

- **Agent headcount** — pricing scales with the number of agents being evaluated
- **Interaction volume** — more conversations = more evaluations = higher consumption
- **Multi-channel complexity** — companies using calls + chat + email need broader coverage
- **Multiple teams / business units** — enterprise rollouts across departments

### Evidence of these characteristics

- Large customer support teams visible on LinkedIn (50+ agents)
- Multiple contact centre locations or BPO partnerships
- Rapid headcount growth in support/CX roles (signals scaling pain)
- High customer base (consumer fintech, marketplaces, SaaS with SMB customers)
- Recent funding round or revenue growth (budget available, scaling operations)

- Industry: Financial services, insurance, fintech, SaaS, e-commerce, healthcare
- Agent headcount: 50+ customer-facing agents
- Tech stack: Zendesk, Aircall, Intercom, Genesys, or similar platforms
- Geography: UK, EU, US, ANZ (regulated markets)
- Company size: 200-5,000 employees (sweet spot for needing dedicated QA but not having built internally)

### Dynamic Signals (who will buy now)

- Recently posted QA-related job openings
- New Head of CX / VP Operations / Director of Quality hire in last 90 days
- Active G2 or Gartner research into QA tools
- Recent compliance incident or regulatory action (public record)
- Funding round in last 6 months (budget unlocked)
- Expanding support team headcount (scaling pain is acute)
